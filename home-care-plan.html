Honest Answers

What's actually included in my membership?

Every tier includes two seasonal inspections with written photo reports, priority scheduling, no emergency call-out fees, member pricing on any repair or project work, and a direct line to our team. Shore and Dual-Home tiers also include seasonal open/close, storm prep before named systems, absence checks, and renter turnover support. Parts and labor beyond routine inspections are billed at member rates. Everything is transparent.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. Month-to-month. No contracts, no cancellation fees. If we're not earning it, you shouldn't pay for it.

I have both a Main Line home and a Shore house. How does that work?

That's the Main Line + Shore tier, and it's honestly who the plan is built for. One team, one point of contact, both homes. You get coordinated care across both properties, shared history so you're never re-explaining your house, and you're first in line when we refer bigger projects. It's the relationship our best clients want.

What does "founding member" mean?

The first 25 members across all tiers get founding pricing, locked in permanently for as long as they stay enrolled. Once those 25 spots fill, standard pricing applies to new enrollments. Founding members never get bumped up. It's a one-time window and it's how we're rewarding the people willing to trust us early.

Can I hire you for a larger project without joining the plan?

No. Our annual project slots are reserved for Home Care Plan members. It's not a sales tactic, it's operational. We take on a small number of bigger projects each year specifically so we can hold the same standard across everything we do. The relationship starts with membership. For larger scopes we don't take on directly, like gut renovations or additions, members get warm introductions to partners we trust.

What happens during a seasonal inspection?

Two to three hours on site. We check HVAC, plumbing shutoffs, water heater, sump pump, exterior caulking, roof from the ground, gutters, deck and railing integrity, grout and sealant, door and window operation, attic moisture, and a few dozen other items. You get a written report with photos within 48 hours.

Is this an insurance product?

No. This is a service membership, not insurance or a home warranty. You're paying for the relationship and the proactive care, not reimbursement when something fails.

Why is this worth it compared to calling someone when things break?

Because reactive home care is more expensive than proactive. Our Shore members have had us catch slow leaks, failed sump pumps, and ice dam setups before they turned into $20k claims. One catch pays for years of membership. On the Main Line, it's mostly about not having to think about your house on a random Tuesday.
